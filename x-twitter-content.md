### Thread 3: "DIY vs. Hiring a Service (Honest Breakdown)"

**Day:** Day 10 (Wednesday)
**Source:** [DIY vs. Service](https://contentrepurposehub.com/blog/content-repurposing-diy-vs-service)

**Tweet 1/8:**
Should you repurpose your webinars yourself or hire someone to do it?

Both options cost you something. Here is the honest comparison -- no spin.

**Tweet 2/8:**
DIY cost breakdown:
- Descript: $24/month
- Canva Pro: $13/month
- Repurpose.io: $29/month
- ChatGPT Plus: $20/month
- CapCut: Free

Total tool cost: ~$100/month.

Sounds cheap. But keep reading.

**Tweet 3/8:**
Time cost per webinar (DIY):
- Transcription + cleanup: 1-2 hrs
- Blog post: 3-4 hrs
- Video clips: 2-3 hrs
- LinkedIn carousel: 1-2 hrs
- Social posts: 1-2 hrs
- Email sequence: 1-2 hrs
- Graphics + scheduling: 2 hrs

Total: 13-22 hours. Per webinar.

**Tweet 4/8:**
At $200/hour, those 15 hours cost $3,000.
At $300/hour, they cost $4,500.

A done-for-you repurposing service costs $750-$1,500/month.

DIY is 2-6x more expensive when you factor in opportunity cost.

**Tweet 5/8:**
Done-for-you service:
- Your time: ~1 hour/month (send recording + review)
- Content output: 14-75+ pieces per webinar
- SEO optimization: Included
- Consistency: Same output every month regardless of how busy you are

**Tweet 6/8:**
The biggest difference nobody talks about: consistency.

DIY works great during slow weeks. Then clients stack up and you have not posted in 12 days. A service delivers whether you are slammed or not. Algorithms reward that.

**Tweet 7/8:**
Here is when each makes sense:

- Billing under $75/hour, calendar not full? DIY.
- $75-$150/hour? Hybrid -- do social yourself, outsource blog + SEO.
- Over $150/hour? Done-for-you. Every hour on content costs more than the service.

**Tweet 8/8:**
There is no universally right answer. A year-one coach with open hours should go DIY. A booked-out coach running 2 webinars a month should not be spending 30 hours on repurposing.

Full comparison with 5-question decision framework:
